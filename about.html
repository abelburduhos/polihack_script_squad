How the system works

Two kinds of climate-driven hazards in Romania are tracked: floods and wildfires. Every wildfire alert is verified by an autonomous drone before any notification leaves the system.

Detection → verification → alert

  1. A mesh of forest sensors continuously polls for signs of ignition. A positive reading creates a pending wildfire marker on the map.
  2. The dispatcher picks the closest idle drone within range whose battery is above the dispatch threshold. If all in-range drones are busy, the report queues at the closest one.
  3. On arrival, the drone scans the area for a few seconds and either confirms or dismisses the report.
  4. Confirmed events notify emergency services. Both confirmed and dismissed reports clear from the map after a configurable retention window.

Configuration

Every parameter — drone range and speed, battery drain rates, sensor counts, retention windows — lives in config.js. Edit the file and reload to apply.

Roadmap

  • Replace simulated sensors with real field hardware.
  • Live data ingestion from the sensor mesh into the map.
  • Multi-drone coordination for parallel verifications.
  • Public alerting for people near a confirmed event.
